Complete Umrah Guide

A comprehensive step-by-step guide to help you prepare for and perform your Umrah pilgrimage.

Pre-Journey Preparation

1. Visa Requirements

  • • Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity)
  • • Umrah visa application
  • • Recent passport-sized photographs
  • • Vaccination certificates (Meningitis, COVID-19)
  • • Mahram requirement for women
  • • Processing time: 7-14 days

2. Spiritual Preparation

  • • Learn Umrah rituals and prayers
  • • Seek forgiveness and make dua
  • • Study Islamic etiquette
  • • Repent from all sins
  • • Memorize essential Arabic phrases
  • • Read about Prophet's Sunnah

3. Packing Essentials

  • • Ihram clothing (white seamless)
  • • Comfortable walking shoes
  • • Personal toiletries (unscented)
  • • Medications and first aid
  • • Tasbih, Quran, prayer mat
  • • Modest clothing for regular wear

Step-by-Step Umrah Rituals

1

Ihram

Entering the State of Ihram

Before crossing the Miqat (designated boundary), perform ghusl, wear ihram clothing, and make the intention (niyyah) for Umrah.

Talbiyah: "Labbaik Allahumma Umratan"
"Here I am, O Allah, for Umrah"
2

Tawaf

Circling the Kaaba

Perform 7 circuits around the Kaaba, starting from the Black Stone. Men should uncover their right shoulder (Idtiba).

  • • Start each circuit by pointing to Black Stone
  • • Recite prayers and dhikr during tawaf
  • • Walk normally, don't push in crowds
  • • Complete 7 full circuits
3

Sa'i

Walking Between Safa and Marwah

Walk between the hills of Safa and Marwah 7 times, following the path of Hajar (AS) in search of water for Ismail (AS).

  • • Start at Safa, end at Marwah
  • • Men should jog between green markers
  • • Make dua during Sa'i
  • • 7 lengths total (not 7 round trips)
4

Halq/Taqsir

Cutting the Hair

Complete the Umrah by cutting your hair. Men can choose between shaving (halq) or trimming (taqsir), women should only trim.

Completion: After this step, you exit ihram and your Umrah is complete. You may now change clothes and resume normal activities.

Essential Do's and Don'ts

DO's

  • Maintain state of purity (wudu) as much as possible
  • Be patient and courteous with fellow pilgrims
  • Keep hydrated and take care of your health
  • Follow your group leader's instructions
  • Make plenty of dua and dhikr
  • Dress modestly and appropriately

DON'Ts

  • Don't argue or fight with anyone
  • Don't use scented products while in ihram
  • Don't cut nails or hair while in ihram
  • Don't engage in intimate relations (for married couples)
  • Don't hunt or harm animals
  • Don't cover your head (men) or face/hands (women)
